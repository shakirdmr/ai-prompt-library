# Job Search Prompts — 50 Free Copy-Paste Examples

## What is this?

This is a collection of 50 prompts for every stage of finding a job — from figuring out what roles to apply for, to cold messaging recruiters, to acing your interview, to evaluating an offer.

Most people search for jobs passively: scroll job boards, send the same resume everywhere, and wait. AI lets you be proactive — writing cold messages, preparing for specific interviews, and practicing answers — all in minutes. These prompts are written to work even if you've never used AI before.

## How to use this

1. Open [ChatGPT](https://chat.openai.com), [Claude](https://claude.ai), or [Gemini](https://gemini.google.com) — all free to use
2. Find the section that matches where you are in your job search
3. Copy the prompt from the code block
4. Replace everything inside `[brackets]` with your real information
5. Paste it into the AI and hit send

> **Tip for beginners:** Start with the "Finding the Right Role" section if you are unsure what jobs to apply for — it is the most overlooked step. Most people jump straight to applying without first being clear on what they actually want or what roles match their skills.

## Finding the Right Role

*Use these before you start applying — clarity on what to search for will save you weeks of wasted applications.*

```
I am a [job title] with [X] years of experience in [skills]. What job titles should I be searching for? Give me 10 variations including ones I might not have considered.
```
> Example: A "social media manager" might also qualify for "content strategist," "digital marketing coordinator," or "brand communications manager." This prompt surfaces those options.

```
What are the top 10 companies hiring [job title] roles in [city / remote] right now? What should I know about each before applying?
```
> Good for: building a targeted company list instead of blindly applying to job boards.

```
I want to transition from [current role] to [target role]. Create a 30-day job search plan with daily actions to maximize my chances.
```
> Good for: career changers who need a structured plan, not just motivation.

```
What are the best job boards for a [job title] in [industry]? Rank them by quality of listings and explain the difference between each.
```
> Good for: beginners who don't know where to look beyond LinkedIn and Indeed.

```
I have been job searching for [X] months with no results. Diagnose what might be going wrong based on my approach: [describe your process]. Give me 5 specific fixes.
```
> Good for: people who are putting in effort but not getting responses — often a resume, outreach, or targeting problem.

## Cold Outreach to Recruiters & Hiring Managers

*Use these to get noticed before a role is even posted — most jobs are filled through outreach and referrals, not job boards.*

```
Write a cold LinkedIn message to a recruiter at [Company] for a [role]. Keep it under 300 characters, show I have done my research, and include a clear ask.
```
> Good for: LinkedIn connection requests — keep them short. Long messages get ignored.

```
Write a cold email to the hiring manager for a [role] at [Company]. Reference something specific about their work or the company, explain why I am a fit, and end with a clear next step. My background: [brief summary].
```
> Good for: going directly to the decision-maker rather than waiting for HR to notice your application.

```
I found an employee at [Company] on LinkedIn who works in the team I want to join. Write a connection message that does not feel like begging for a job but opens a conversation.
```
> Good for: warm networking that builds a relationship first, then opens the door naturally.

```
Write a follow-up email after submitting a job application 2 weeks ago. Keep it brief, polite, and show continued interest without being pushy.
```
> Good for: the follow-up most candidates forget to send — it can make the difference between being remembered and being ignored.

```
Write a thank-you email after a first-round interview at [Company] for a [role]. Reference one specific thing we discussed and reaffirm my interest.
```
> Good for: standing out after an interview — most candidates do not send a follow-up at all.

## Interview Preparation

*Use these to prepare for specific interviews so you walk in confident instead of winging it.*

```
I have an interview for a [role] at [Company]. Give me the 10 most likely interview questions and write strong STAR-method answers for each based on my background: [paste resume or brief summary].
```
> This is the most valuable prompt in this list. Give ChatGPT your actual resume and the company name — the answers it generates will be tailored to your experience.

```
What are the most common behavioral interview questions for a [role]? Write model answers using the STAR method.
```
> Good for: behavioral questions like "Tell me about a time you handled a conflict" — these trip up most candidates who haven't prepared.

```
I have a technical interview for a [role]. What topics should I study and what types of problems are commonly asked at [Company or company type]?
```
> Good for: software engineering, data, finance, or any role with a technical screening round.

```
Help me prepare for a case study interview at a consulting firm. Walk me through a mock case about [topic] and give me feedback on my structure.
```
> Good for: McKinsey, BCG, Deloitte, or any firm that uses case interviews — one of the hardest interview formats to prepare for alone.

```
Write 10 smart questions I should ask at the end of my interview for a [role] at [Company]. Make them show strategic thinking and genuine curiosity.
```
> Good for: the "Do you have any questions for us?" moment — the questions you ask tell the interviewer as much about you as the answers you give.

## Handling Difficult Interview Questions

*Use these to prepare honest, confident answers for the questions most people fumble.*

```
I was laid off from my last job. Write a confident, honest answer to "Why did you leave your last role?" that does not raise red flags.
```
> Good for: layoffs are very common — a prepared, calm answer removes any awkwardness immediately.

```
I have a 2-year employment gap. Write a clear, positive answer to explain it in an interview for a [role].
```
> Good for: gaps from caregiving, health, travel, studying, or any other reason — framed correctly, they are not a dealbreaker.

```
Write an answer to "What is your greatest weakness?" for a [job title] interview that is honest but does not hurt my candidacy.
```
> Good for: one of the most asked and most poorly answered interview questions. The key is choosing a real weakness that you are actively working on.

```
I am switching careers from [old field] to [new field]. Write an answer to "Why are you making this change?" that sounds intentional and not desperate.
```
> Good for: career changers — you need to frame the change as a deliberate move toward something, not running away from your old job.

```
Write an answer to "Where do you see yourself in 5 years?" for someone applying to a [role] who genuinely is unsure but wants to sound motivated.
```
> Good for: the classic question most people either answer too vaguely or too rigidly.

## LinkedIn Profile Optimization

*Use these to turn your LinkedIn profile from a resume copy into a page that attracts recruiters to you.*

```
Rewrite my LinkedIn headline for a [job title] who is actively looking for [type of role]. Make it keyword-rich and stand out in search results. Current: [paste headline].
```
> Good for: most LinkedIn headlines say something like "Student at X University" or "Open to Work" — this prompt helps you write something that actually gets found.

```
I want to signal that I am open to new opportunities on LinkedIn without broadcasting it to my current employer. Write the best approach and example text for my profile.
```
> Good for: anyone who is job searching quietly while still employed.

```
Write a LinkedIn post announcing I am open to work as a [job title]. Make it confident, specific about what I am looking for, and easy for people to share or tag others.
```
> Good for: announcing your job search publicly to activate your network — one post can generate 10 referrals.

```
What are the 5 most important sections of a LinkedIn profile for someone searching for a [role]? What should be in each section?
```
> Good for: complete beginners who are setting up or overhauling their LinkedIn profile.

```
My LinkedIn profile gets low views. Diagnose the problem and give me 5 specific improvements: [paste your headline, summary, and one experience entry].
```
> Good for: people who have a profile but are not showing up in recruiter searches.

## Networking

*Use these to build genuine connections that lead to referrals — the fastest way to get hired.*

```
I want to build my professional network to find jobs through referrals. Write a 30-day LinkedIn networking plan for a [job title] targeting [industry].
```
> Good for: people who know networking matters but don't have a system.

```
Write a script for a 20-minute informational interview call with someone who works at [Company]. What should I ask to learn about the team and increase my chances of getting referred?
```
> Good for: informational interviews are the most underused job search tool — a 20-minute call often leads directly to a referral.

```
I want to reconnect with a former colleague who works at a company I want to join. Write a message that does not feel transactional and opens naturally.
```
> Good for: warm outreach to people you already know — far easier than cold messaging.

```
Write a thank-you message after someone referred me for a job at their company. Keep it genuine and let them know I submitted the application.
```
> Good for: closing the loop with your referrer — it builds the relationship and they will be more likely to advocate for you internally.

```
I attended a networking event and met [Name] who works at [Company]. Write a follow-up LinkedIn connection message that references our conversation and moves the relationship forward.
```
> Good for: following up after events before you are forgotten — send within 24 hours.

## Offer Evaluation

*Use these before you sign anything — most people focus only on salary and miss the things that matter long-term.*

```
I received two job offers: [Offer A details] and [Offer B details]. Help me build a structured comparison framework to decide which to take, including factors beyond salary.
```
> Good for: comparing offers holistically — growth, culture, flexibility, and equity matter as much as base salary.

```
What questions should I ask before accepting a job offer to avoid surprises in the first 90 days?
```
> Good for: anyone who has ever started a new job and discovered something important that wasn't mentioned during the interview.

```
I want to accept a job offer but ask for a later start date. Write a professional email requesting to start on [date] instead of [date].
```
> Good for: if you need time to wrap up your current role, take a break, or handle personal matters before starting.

```
Write a professional email to decline a job offer politely while keeping the relationship warm in case I want to work there in the future.
```
> Good for: declining gracefully — the hiring manager might be your future colleague, client, or reference.

```
I accepted a job offer verbally but I got a better offer the next day. Write a professional, honest email to withdraw from the first offer without burning the bridge.
```
> Good for: an awkward situation that happens more than people admit — handled honestly and promptly, it is rarely as damaging as feared.
