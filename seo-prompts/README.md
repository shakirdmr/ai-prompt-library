# SEO Prompts — 10 Deep Copy-Paste Examples

## What is this?

This is a collection of deep, structured prompts for doing SEO work with **ChatGPT** or **Claude** — from keyword research to writing meta descriptions, auditing a page, building a content strategy, and understanding your Google Search Console data.

SEO used to require expensive agencies or months of learning. With AI, you can do the same work in minutes — if you ask the right way. These prompts are written the way an experienced SEO consultant would approach each task. You fill in your details, and the AI does the heavy lifting.

## How to use this

1. Open [ChatGPT](https://chat.openai.com) or [Claude](https://claude.ai) — both free to use
2. Find the prompt that matches your SEO task
3. Copy the full prompt from the code block
4. Replace everything inside `[brackets]` with your real information
5. Paste and send — then follow up with questions to go deeper

> **Tip for beginners:** SEO feels overwhelming because there are hundreds of things you *could* do. Start with prompt **#1** (keyword research) and **#3** (on-page audit) — these two alone will tell you what to focus on first.

**1.**
```
ROLE: You are a senior SEO strategist with 10 years of experience growing organic traffic for small and medium-sized businesses. You specialise in finding low-competition keywords that beginners can actually rank for.

CONTEXT: My website is [website URL or brief description]. It is about [what your site does]. My target audience is [who you are trying to reach]. I am currently getting [rough traffic number or "almost no"] visitors from Google.

TASK: Give me a keyword research plan for my website. Specifically:
1. Identify 5 high-intent, low-competition keywords I could realistically rank for in the next 3–6 months
2. For each keyword, explain: the search intent (what the user actually wants), why it is achievable, and what type of content would rank for it
3. Flag which 1–2 keywords I should target first and why
4. Suggest 3 "long-tail" variations of the top keyword (longer, more specific phrases with less competition)

CONSTRAINTS: Focus on keywords a beginner website can rank for — not head terms dominated by Wikipedia or major brands. Prioritise buyer or problem-aware intent over informational intent where possible.
```
*Builds your entire keyword strategy from scratch — gives you a prioritised list of keywords you can actually rank for, not just popular ones you'll never beat.*

&nbsp;

**2.**
```
ROLE: You are an SEO content brief writer. Your briefs are used by writers to create blog posts that rank on page 1 of Google.

CONTEXT: I want to write a blog post targeting the keyword "[your target keyword]". My website is about [what your site does]. My audience is [describe your reader — their experience level, what they are trying to do].

TASK: Write a complete SEO content brief for this keyword. Include:
1. Recommended title (H1) — include the keyword naturally, make it click-worthy
2. Meta description — 150–160 characters, includes keyword, gives a clear reason to click
3. Suggested URL slug — short, keyword-focused
4. Content outline — H2 and H3 headings that cover the topic thoroughly
5. Word count recommendation
6. 3 related keywords to include naturally throughout the article
7. What the top-ranking articles are likely covering that I must also cover (based on what users expect for this keyword)
8. One thing I can include that would make my article more useful than the typical result

CONSTRAINTS: The outline should answer the search intent completely — not just mention the topic. Every H2 should represent a genuine question or need the reader has.
```
*Gives you a full blueprint for a blog post before you write a single word — so the article is built for ranking from the start, not optimised after the fact.*

&nbsp;

**3.**
```
ROLE: You are a technical and on-page SEO auditor. You identify exactly what is stopping a page from ranking higher and give a prioritised action list.

CONTEXT: Here is my page:
- URL: [paste your URL]
- Target keyword: [the keyword you want this page to rank for]
- Current position on Google (if known): [position or "not ranking yet"]
- Page title: [paste your current title tag]
- Meta description: [paste your current meta description]
- H1: [paste your H1]
- First 100 words of the page content: [paste]

TASK: Audit this page for on-page SEO. Give me:
1. Title tag — is it optimised? Rewrite it if not (max 60 characters, keyword near the front)
2. Meta description — rewrite it to be click-worthy (150–160 chars, includes keyword, has a clear benefit or hook)
3. H1 — does it match search intent? Suggest an improvement if needed
4. Content assessment — based on the first 100 words, what is missing? What should I add or restructure?
5. Quick wins — 3 changes I can make today that will have the most impact
6. One thing that is probably causing this page to underperform

CONSTRAINTS: Be specific. Do not give me general advice — tell me exactly what to change and what to change it to.
```
*Acts as a professional SEO audit on a single page — gives you a prioritised punch list of exactly what to fix, not vague advice like "improve your content."*

&nbsp;

**4.**
```
ROLE: You are an SEO copywriter who specialises in writing title tags and meta descriptions that get high click-through rates from Google search results.

CONTEXT:
- Page topic: [what the page is about]
- Target keyword: [the main keyword]
- Page type: [blog post / product page / landing page / home page]
- Unique value this page offers: [what makes your page worth clicking on]

TASK: Write 5 variations of the title tag and meta description for this page.

For each variation:
- Title tag: max 60 characters, keyword included, click-worthy
- Meta description: 150–160 characters, keyword included, ends with an implicit or explicit reason to click

After the 5 variations, tell me which one you recommend and why — based on likely click-through rate and keyword alignment.

CONSTRAINTS: Do not start every title with the keyword — vary the structure. No clickbait. The description must accurately reflect what is on the page.
```
*Writes 5 ready-to-use title and meta description options so you can pick the strongest one — the most direct way to improve your Google click-through rate.*

&nbsp;

**5.**
```
ROLE: You are a Google Search Console data analyst who helps website owners understand what their data means and what to do next.

CONTEXT: Here is my Google Search Console performance data for the last 3 months:
[Paste your top queries, impressions, clicks, and average position — or describe what you see: "I have 5,000 impressions but only 20 clicks" / "My top query is X with 200 impressions at position 14"]

My website is about: [brief description]

TASK: Analyse this data and tell me:
1. What is the biggest opportunity I am currently missing?
2. Which pages or queries should I prioritise improving first and why?
3. What does my average position tell me — am I close to page 1 on anything?
4. If I have high impressions but low clicks — what is likely wrong and how do I fix it?
5. Give me a 3-step action plan based specifically on this data

CONSTRAINTS: Speak plainly. I am not an SEO expert. Explain what each metric means before telling me what to do about it.
```
*Turns confusing Google Search Console numbers into a plain-English action plan — so you know exactly which pages to improve and in what order.*

&nbsp;

**6.**
```
ROLE: You are an internal linking strategist. You understand that internal links are one of the most underused SEO levers available to small website owners.

CONTEXT: My website has the following pages (list your key pages with their topics and URLs):
[List 5–15 pages: e.g. "Homepage — promptitin.com", "Blog: How to write better ChatGPT prompts — /blog/chatgpt-prompts", "Guide: What is prompt engineering — /guides/what-is-prompt-engineering"]

My most important page (the one I most want to rank) is: [URL and target keyword]

TASK: Build an internal linking plan for my website. Tell me:
1. Which pages should link TO my most important page, and what anchor text to use for each link
2. Are there any pages that are likely "orphaned" (no links pointing to them)? How do I fix that?
3. What is the ideal internal linking structure for a site like mine?
4. Give me 5 specific sentences I can add to existing pages that include a natural internal link — write the actual sentence, not a template

CONSTRAINTS: Anchor text should be natural and keyword-relevant, not just "click here." Every suggested link should make sense to a real reader.
```
*Builds a complete internal linking plan that passes authority between your pages — one of the highest-ROI SEO tasks that most beginners completely ignore.*

**7.**
```
ROLE: You are a competitor SEO analyst. You reverse-engineer what competitors are doing to rank well and identify the gaps a new site can exploit.

CONTEXT: My website is about [your topic/niche]. My main competitors (pages that rank above me for my target keywords) are:
- [Competitor 1 URL]
- [Competitor 2 URL]
- [Competitor 3 URL if you have one]

My target keyword is: [keyword]
My current page targeting this keyword: [your URL, or "I don't have one yet"]

TASK: Based on what you know about these competitors and their likely content strategy:
1. What are they probably doing well that explains their rankings? (content depth, authority, structure)
2. What gaps or weaknesses can I exploit? What are they NOT covering well?
3. What is one angle or content format they are all missing that I could own?
4. How long and how detailed does my content need to be to compete?
5. What would my page need to have that theirs don't, to give Google a reason to rank mine higher?

CONSTRAINTS: Focus on actionable differentiation. I need a realistic path to ranking — not a list of things I can never compete on.
```
*Reverse-engineers why your competitors rank and finds the specific gaps you can exploit — so you build content that has a real chance of outranking them.*

**8.**
```
ROLE: You are an SEO content writer who specialises in writing blog introductions that rank well and keep readers on the page.

CONTEXT:
- Article title: [your title]
- Target keyword: [keyword]
- Reader: [describe who is reading this — their problem, experience level, what they want]
- Key point of the article: [what the article teaches or solves]

TASK: Write 3 versions of the blog post introduction (first 100–150 words each).

Each version should:
- Include the target keyword in the first or second sentence naturally
- Hook the reader immediately — address their problem or promise a specific result
- Preview what the article covers so the reader knows they are in the right place
- Be written at a clear, conversational level — no jargon

After the 3 versions, tell me which one is strongest for SEO and reader retention, and why.

CONSTRAINTS: No generic openers like "In today's digital world..." or "Many people wonder about...". Start with the problem, a surprising fact, or a direct statement.
```
*Writes 3 ready-to-use blog introductions that hook readers and signal relevance to Google — the first 150 words of a blog post determine whether people stay or bounce.*

&nbsp;

**9.**
```
ROLE: You are a local SEO specialist who helps small businesses appear in Google Maps results and local searches like "[service] near me" or "[service] in [city]."

CONTEXT:
- Business name: [your business name]
- Business type: [what you do — e.g. "Italian restaurant", "plumber", "personal trainer"]
- Location: [city and neighbourhood]
- Current Google Business Profile: [Do you have one? Is it filled out? Any reviews?]
- Main keywords you want to appear for: [e.g. "best pizza in Manchester" or "plumber in Brooklyn"]

TASK: Give me a local SEO action plan. Tell me:
1. What are the 3 most important things I should do this week to improve my local rankings?
2. How should I optimise my Google Business Profile — what specifically should I add or change?
3. What should my homepage title tag and meta description say to target local searches?
4. How do I get more Google reviews, and do they actually affect rankings?
5. What are local citations and which ones matter most for my business type?

CONSTRAINTS: Assume I have limited time. Prioritise the actions with the highest impact first. Explain each step as if I have never done SEO before.
```
*Builds a local SEO plan for businesses that want to appear in Google Maps and "near me" searches — the most important SEO channel for any local business.*

**10.**
```
ROLE: You are an SEO strategist who helps new websites build their first 6 months of organic traffic from zero.

CONTEXT:
- My website: [URL or description]
- What it does: [your product, service, or content topic]
- My goal: [e.g. "get 1,000 visitors/month", "rank for [keyword]", "generate leads for my service"]
- Current status: [e.g. "just launched", "3 months old, almost no traffic", "have 5 blog posts but no rankings"]
- Time I can invest: [e.g. "2 hours per week", "full time"]

TASK: Build me a 6-month SEO roadmap. Include:
1. Month 1–2: What to set up and fix first (technical foundations, Google Search Console, sitemap)
2. Month 2–4: Content to create — what types, how many, targeting which keywords
3. Month 4–6: Building authority — how to get backlinks as a small site with no connections
4. What metrics to track each month so I know if I am making progress
5. The single biggest mistake new websites make with SEO — and how I avoid it

CONSTRAINTS: Be realistic about timelines. Do not promise rankings — explain what consistent effort can achieve. Prioritise actions that compound over time.
```
*Gives you a full 6-month SEO roadmap from scratch — so instead of doing random SEO tasks, you have a clear sequence that builds on itself month by month.*
